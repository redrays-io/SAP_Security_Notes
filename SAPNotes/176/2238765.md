### **Description**
**Symptom:**  
CacheAPITester, CacheTimestampTest, and findMerge can be exploited by an attacker to modify displayed application content without authorization and potentially obtain authentication information from other legitimate users.

**CVSS Information:**  
- **Base Score:** 6.1 / 10  
- **Vector:**  
  - **Attack Vector:** Network (N)  
  - **Attack Complexity:** Low (L)  
  - **Privileges Required:** None (N)  
  - **User Interaction:** Required (R)  
  - **Scope:** Changed (C)  
  - **Confidentiality Impact:** Low (L)  
  - **Integrity Impact:** Low (L)  
  - **Availability Impact:** None (N)

**Reason and Prerequisites:**  
The functions CacheAPITester, CacheTimestampTest, and findMerge do not sufficiently encode OUTPUT parameters, leading to a reflected cross-site scripting (XSS) vulnerability. This can be exploited to deface or modify web content temporarily and steal users' authentication information.
